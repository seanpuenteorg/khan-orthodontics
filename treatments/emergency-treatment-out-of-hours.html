EMERGENCY TREATMENT OUT OF HOURS

About This Treatment

Dental emergencies don't follow office hours — a cracked tooth, a lost crown, a swollen abscess, or sudden severe pain can happen on a Sunday evening or a bank holiday, and waiting until Monday morning isn't always an option. Khan Dental Liverpool offers out-of-hours emergency appointments for patients who need urgent care when other practices are closed. Whether you're an existing patient or someone in Liverpool who needs help fast, Dr Khan's practice provides direct access to a dentist — not a call centre or an NHS 111 referral. Emergency treatment may include pain relief, temporary or permanent restorations, drainage of infections, or stabilisation of a broken tooth.

Frequently Asked Questions

A dental emergency is any situation where you're in significant pain, have visible swelling (particularly around the jaw or neck), have suffered trauma to a tooth, or have lost a restoration that's left a tooth sensitive or sharp. Swelling that spreads to the neck or causes difficulty swallowing or breathing is a medical emergency requiring A&E attendance immediately. For everything else — severe toothache, a broken tooth, a lost crown, a knocked-out tooth — contacting Khan Dental Liverpool directly is the right first step.

Pick the tooth up by the crown (the white part), not the root, and do not scrub or rinse it aggressively. If possible, place it back in the socket immediately and hold it there — this gives the best chance of successful reimplantation. If you can't do that, store it in milk or between your cheek and gum to keep it moist, and get to a dentist within 30 to 60 minutes. The survival rate of a reimplanted tooth drops significantly after an hour outside the mouth.

A dental abscess with facial swelling needs to be seen urgently — do not wait and hope it resolves on its own, as dental infections can spread to the jaw, neck, and airway. If the swelling is affecting your ability to open your mouth, swallow, or breathe, go to A&E immediately. For localised swelling around a tooth or gum, contact Khan Dental Liverpool for an emergency appointment — treatment typically involves draining the abscess, prescribing antibiotics, and addressing the source tooth.
